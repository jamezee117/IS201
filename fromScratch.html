The Restoration Gorongosa National Park

Gorongosa National Park in Mozambique is one of Africa’s greatest conservation success stories. After decades of civil conflict devastated wildlife populations, a massive restoration effort began to rebuild, or in some cases reintroduce completely, the population of lions, wild dogs, elephants, hippos, monkeys, and antelope. The restoration work is still ongoing with plans being drawn up to restore giraffers and rhinos to the park as well.

How Gorongosa Was Rebuilt

  1. Wildlife Restoration
    • Reintroducing lions and other predators
    • Protecting elephant herds
    • Rebuilding antelope populations
  2. Community Partnership
    • Local employment programs
    • Girls’ education initiatives
    • Sustainable agriculture training
  3. Scientific Research
    • Rainforest biodiversity studies
    • Tracking animal migrations
    • Climate resilience projects
